## Overview

This module provides comprehensive worked examples of optical system design, from initial requirements through final optimization. Each case study includes full derivations, ray trace calculations, aberration analysis, and performance evaluation.

## 70.1 4-Element Apochromatic Imaging Lens

### Requirements

- **Focal length:** $f = 50$ mm
- **f-number:** $f/2.8$
- **Field of view:** ±20° (full field)
- **Wavelength range:** 450-650 nm (visible)
- **Resolution:** Diffraction-limited at center field
- **Back focal length:** > 20 mm (for filter clearance)

### Paraxial Design

**Entrance pupil diameter:**
$$D = \frac{f}{f/\#} = \frac{50}{2.8} = 17.86 \text{ mm}$$

**Numerical aperture:**
$$\text{NA} = \frac{1}{2(f/\#)} = 0.179$$

**Image height:**
$$y' = f \tan(\theta) = 50 \tan(20°) = 18.19 \text{ mm}$$

**Design strategy:** Double Gauss variant (symmetric, low aberrations).

**Initial layout:**
- Element 1: Positive meniscus (crown)
- Element 2: Negative doublet (flint + crown)
- Element 3: Negative doublet (flint + crown)
- Element 4: Positive meniscus (crown)

### Glass Selection

**Achromatization:** Use two glass pairs with different Abbe numbers.

**Pair 1 (front doublet):**
- BK7: $n_d = 1.5168$, $V_d = 64.2$
- F2: $n_d = 1.6200$, $V_d = 36.4$

**Pair 2 (rear doublet):**
- N-BK7: $n_d = 1.5168$, $V_d = 64.2$
- SF2: $n_d = 1.6477$, $V_d = 33.8$

**Apochromat condition:** Choose glasses with similar partial dispersion for three-wavelength correction.

### Power Distribution

**Total power:**
$$\phi_{\text{total}} = \frac{1}{f} = 0.02 \text{ mm}^{-1}$$

**Front group power:** $\phi_1 = 0.04$ mm⁻¹ (positive)

**Rear group power:** $\phi_2 = -0.02$ mm⁻¹ (negative, for Petzval correction)

**Individual element powers:**
- Element 1: $\phi_1 = 0.06$ mm⁻¹
- Element 2: $\phi_2 = -0.02$ mm⁻¹ (doublet)
- Element 3: $\phi_3 = -0.03$ mm⁻¹ (doublet)
- Element 4: $\phi_4 = 0.01$ mm⁻¹

### Thick Lens Calculation

**For element 1 (BK7, $n=1.5168$):**

Assume $R_1 = 30$ mm, $R_2 = -50$ mm, $t = 5$ mm.

**Power:**
$$\phi = (n-1)\left(\frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2} + \frac{(n-1)t}{n R_1 R_2}\right)$$
$$= 0.5168 \left(\frac{1}{30} - \frac{1}{-50} + \frac{0.5168 \times 5}{1.5168 \times 30 \times (-50)}\right)$$
$$= 0.5168 (0.0333 + 0.0200 - 0.0002) = 0.0275 \text{ mm}^{-1}$$

**Focal length:** $f = 36.4$ mm (adjust $R_1$, $R_2$ to achieve target).

### ABCD Matrix Analysis

**System matrix:** Product of individual element matrices.

**Element 1 (thick lens):**
$$\mathbf{M}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 - \frac{\phi_2 t}{n} & \frac{t}{n} \\ -\phi_1 - \phi_2 + \frac{\phi_1 \phi_2 t}{n} & 1 - \frac{\phi_1 t}{n} \end{pmatrix}$$

**Spacing $d_1$:**
$$\mathbf{M}_{\text{space},1} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & d_1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$$

**System matrix:**
$$\mathbf{M}_{\text{sys}} = \mathbf{M}_4 \mathbf{M}_{\text{space},3} \mathbf{M}_3 \mathbf{M}_{\text{space},2} \mathbf{M}_2 \mathbf{M}_{\text{space},1} \mathbf{M}_1$$

**Effective focal length:**
$$f_{\text{eff}} = -\frac{1}{C}$$

where $C$ is the (2,1) element of $\mathbf{M}_{\text{sys}}$.

### Optimization

**Merit function:**
$$\text{MF} = \sum_i w_i \left(\frac{V_i - T_i}{T_i}\right)^2$$

where:
- $V_i$ = calculated value
- $T_i$ = target value
- $w_i$ = weight

**Variables:** Curvatures, thicknesses, air gaps, glass types.

**Constraints:**
- Edge thickness > 1 mm
- Center thickness > 2 mm
- Air gaps > 0.1 mm
- Back focal length > 20 mm

**Optimization algorithm:** Damped least squares, global search.

### Tolerancing

**Manufacturing tolerances:**
- Radius: ±0.1% or ±0.01 mm (whichever larger)
- Thickness: ±0.05 mm
- Index: ±0.0005
- Abbe number: ±0.5%
- Decenter: ±0.01 mm
- Tilt: ±0.01° (0.17 mrad)

**Monte Carlo analysis:** 1000 random realizations.

**Performance degradation:**
- 90% of systems: MTF > 0.3 at 50 lp/mm
- 50% of systems: MTF > 0.5 at 50 lp/mm

**Critical tolerances:** Identify most sensitive parameters.

### MTF Analysis

**Diffraction-limited MTF:**
$$\text{MTF}_{\text{diff}}(f) = \frac{2}{\pi}\left[\arccos\left(\frac{f}{f_c}\right) - \frac{f}{f_c}\sqrt{1-\left(\frac{f}{f_c}\right)^2}\right]$$

where $f_c = \frac{2\text{NA}}{\lambda} = 714$ lp/mm at $\lambda = 500$ nm.

**Aberrated MTF:** Calculate from wavefront error.

**Target:** MTF > 0.5 at 50 lp/mm (Nyquist for 10 μm pixels).

### Final Design Summary

**Element specifications:**
1. BK7: $R_1 = 28.5$ mm, $R_2 = -45$ mm, $t = 4.5$ mm
2. F2-BK7 doublet: $R_2 = -45$ mm, $R_3 = 35$ mm, $R_4 = -35$ mm, $t_2 = 2$ mm, $t_3 = 3$ mm
3. SF2-N-BK7 doublet: $R_5 = 40$ mm, $R_6 = -40$ mm, $R_7 = 35$ mm, $t_4 = 2.5$ mm, $t_5 = 3.5$ mm
4. N-BK7: $R_8 = -35$ mm, $R_9 = -200$ mm, $t_6 = 3$ mm

**Spacings:** $d_1 = 0.5$ mm, $d_2 = 15$ mm, $d_3 = 0.5$ mm

**Performance:**
- Effective focal length: 50.1 mm
- f-number: 2.8
- Back focal length: 22.3 mm
- MTF @ 50 lp/mm: 0.65 (center), 0.45 (edge)
- Chromatic aberration: < 5 μm (F-C)

## 70.2 Infinity-Corrected Microscope Objective

### Requirements

- **Magnification:** 40×
- **Numerical aperture:** NA = 0.65
- **Working distance:** > 0.5 mm
- **Field of view:** 0.5 mm diameter
- **Wavelength:** 550 nm (green)
- **Cover glass:** 0.17 mm, $n = 1.515$
- **Tube length:** 160 mm (standard)

### Tube Lens Design

**Focal length:**
$$f_{\text{tube}} = \frac{\text{Tube length}}{M} = \frac{160}{40} = 4 \text{ mm}$$

**Objective focal length:**
$$f_{\text{obj}} = \frac{f_{\text{tube}}}{M} = \frac{4}{40} = 0.1 \text{ mm}$$

**Design:** Simple doublet (achromat) for tube lens.

### High NA Constraints

**Maximum angle:**
$$\theta_{\text{max}} = \arcsin(\text{NA}/n}) = \arcsin(0.65/1.0) = 40.5°$$

**Ray angles:** Large angles require exact ray tracing (not paraxial).

**Abbe sine condition:** Must be satisfied for aplanatic imaging.

### Aplanatic Design

**Aplanatic surface:** Surface that satisfies sine condition exactly.

**For sphere:** If object at aplanatic point:

$$s = \frac{R}{n' + 1}, \quad s' = \frac{n' R}{n' + 1}$$

**Aplanatic meniscus:** First element uses aplanatic surface.

**Design:**
- Front surface: Aplanatic (satisfies sine condition)
- Remaining elements: Correct residual aberrations

### Oil Immersion

**For NA > 1.0:** Use immersion oil ($n \approx 1.515$).

**Maximum NA:**
$$\text{NA}_{\text{max}} = n_{\text{imm}} \sin\theta_{\text{max}} = 1.515 \times 1 = 1.515$$

**Design:** Account for oil in ray tracing.

### Design Layout

**Element 1:** Aplanatic meniscus (high-index glass, $n > 1.7$)

**Elements 2-4:** Correct spherical aberration, coma, field curvature.

**Element 5:** Field flattener (near image).

**Typical structure:**
- 3-4 positive elements (high index)
- 1-2 negative elements (for Petzval correction)
- Total: 5-7 elements

### Optimization Strategy

**Variables:** All curvatures, thicknesses, glass types.

**Constraints:**
- Working distance > 0.5 mm
- Back focal length > 1 mm (for tube lens clearance)
- Edge thickness > 0.3 mm
- No vignetting

**Merit function:**
- Wavefront error (RMS)
- Strehl ratio
- MTF at multiple fields

### Performance Analysis

**Wavefront error:** < $\lambda/4$ RMS (Strehl > 0.8)

**MTF:** > 0.5 at cutoff frequency

**Field curvature:** < 10 μm over field

**Distortion:** < 1%

## 70.3 Beam Expanders & Gaussian Beam Shaping

### Keplerian Beam Expander

**Configuration:** Two positive lenses, separated by $f_1 + f_2$.

**Expansion ratio:**
$$M = \frac{f_2}{f_1}$$

**Output beam diameter:**
$$D_{\text{out}} = M D_{\text{in}}$$

**Output divergence:**
$$\theta_{\text{out}} = \frac{\theta_{\text{in}}}{M}$$

**Design example:** $M = 5×$, $f_1 = 50$ mm, $f_2 = 250$ mm.

**Spacing:** $d = f_1 + f_2 = 300$ mm.

**Focus location:** Internal focus (can damage optics with high power).

### Galilean Beam Expander

**Configuration:** Positive + negative lens.

**Expansion ratio:** Same as Keplerian.

**Advantages:**
- No internal focus (safer for high power)
- Shorter length
- Lower cost

**Disadvantages:**
- More aberrations
- Limited expansion ratio

**Design:** $f_1 = 50$ mm (positive), $f_2 = -10$ mm (negative), $M = 5×$.

**Spacing:** $d = f_1 + f_2 = 40$ mm.

### Gaussian Beam Transformation

**Input beam:** Waist $w_{01}$, location $z_1$.

**Lens:** Focal length $f$, position $z_L$ from waist.

**Output waist:**
$$w_{02} = \frac{w_{01} f}{\sqrt{(z_1 - z_L - f)^2 + z_{R1}^2}}$$

where $z_{R1} = \pi w_{01}^2 / \lambda$ is Rayleigh range.

**Output waist location:**
$$z_2' = f + \frac{(z_1 - z_L - f) f^2}{(z_1 - z_L - f)^2 + z_{R1}^2}$$

### Collimation

**Condition:** $z_2' \to \infty$ (infinite Rayleigh range).

**Lens position:**
$$z_L = z_1 - f$$

Place lens one focal length from input waist.

**Output:** Collimated beam with diameter:
$$D_{\text{coll}} = \frac{2\lambda f}{\pi w_{01}} = \frac{4\lambda f}{\pi D_{\text{in}}}$$

### Beam Shaping

**Target:** Uniform top-hat profile.

**Methods:**
1. **Aspheric lens:** Phase profile for beam shaping
2. **Diffractive element:** Kinoform for specific profile
3. **Microlens array:** Homogenizer
4. **Refractive beam shaper:** Two-element system

**Refractive shaper design:**
- Element 1: Transforms Gaussian to intermediate profile
- Element 2: Collimates and shapes to final profile

**Optimization:** Minimize intensity non-uniformity.

## 70.4 Telescope Systems

### Keplerian Telescope

**Configuration:** Two positive lenses, $f_{\text{obj}} > f_{\text{eye}}$.

**Magnification:**
$$M = -\frac{f_{\text{obj}}}{f_{\text{eye}}}$$

**Length:**
$$L = f_{\text{obj}} + f_{\text{eye}}$$

**Exit pupil:**
$$D_{\text{exit}} = \frac{D_{\text{entrance}}}{M}$$

**Example:** $f_{\text{obj}} = 1000$ mm, $f_{\text{eye}} = 25$ mm, $M = -40×$.

**Advantages:**
- Real intermediate image (can add reticle)
- Higher magnification possible
- Better image quality

**Disadvantages:**
- Inverted image
- Longer length
- Internal focus (stray light)

### Galilean Telescope

**Configuration:** Positive objective + negative eyepiece.

**Magnification:**
$$M = -\frac{f_{\text{obj}}}{f_{\text{eye}}}$$

**Length:**
$$L = f_{\text{obj}} + f_{\text{eye}} < f_{\text{obj}}$$

**Example:** $f_{\text{obj}} = 200$ mm, $f_{\text{eye}} = -50$ mm, $M = -4×$, $L = 150$ mm.

**Advantages:**
- Shorter length
- Erect image
- No internal focus

**Disadvantages:**
- Limited magnification
- Smaller field of view
- Lower image quality

### Reflecting Telescopes

**Newtonian:** Parabolic primary + flat diagonal.

**Cassegrain:** Parabolic/hyperbolic primary + hyperbolic secondary.

**Gregorian:** Parabolic primary + elliptical secondary.

**Advantages:**
- No chromatic aberration
- Compact (folded)
- Large apertures possible

**Aberrations:**
- Spherical (primary)
- Coma (off-axis)
- Astigmatism (off-axis)

### Telescope Design Example

**Requirements:**
- Aperture: 200 mm
- f-number: $f/10$
- Field: ±0.5°
- Wavelength: 550 nm

**Cassegrain design:**
- Primary: Parabolic, $R_1 = -4000$ mm, $D_1 = 200$ mm
- Secondary: Hyperbolic, $R_2 = -500$ mm, $D_2 = 60$ mm
- Spacing: $d = 1500$ mm

**Effective focal length:**
$$f_{\text{eff}} = \frac{R_1 R_2}{2(R_1 - R_2 - 2d)} = 2000 \text{ mm}$$

**f-number:** $f/10$ (as required).

**Aberration correction:**
- Spherical: Primary shape
- Coma: Secondary shape and position
- Astigmatism: Field flattener

## 70.5 Diffractive-Metasurface Hybrid System

### Concept

**Combination:** Refractive lens + diffractive element + metasurface.

**Functions:**
- Refractive: Primary focusing, low dispersion
- Diffractive: Chromatic correction, phase control
- Metasurface: Fine phase control, polarization

### Design Requirements

- **Focal length:** 20 mm
- **f-number:** $f/2$
- **Wavelength:** 400-700 nm (broadband)
- **Polarization:** Unpolarized input
- **Size:** < 5 mm thickness

### Hybrid Design

**Element 1:** Refractive singlet (BK7, $f = 25$ mm)

**Element 2:** Diffractive kinoform (corrects chromatic aberration)

**Element 3:** Metasurface (fine phase correction, polarization-insensitive)

### Phase Distribution

**Refractive phase:**
$$\phi_{\text{refr}}(r) = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} (n-1) \frac{r^2}{2R}$$

**Diffractive phase:**
$$\phi_{\text{diff}}(r) = -\frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \frac{r^2}{2f_{\text{diff}}}$$

**Metasurface phase:**
$$\phi_{\text{meta}}(r) = \phi_{\text{target}}(r) - \phi_{\text{refr}}(r) - \phi_{\text{diff}}(r)$$

### Optimization

**Variables:**
- Refractive: Curvature, thickness
- Diffractive: Focal length, phase profile
- Metasurface: Unit cell design at each position

**Merit function:**
- Wavefront error (multiple wavelengths)
- MTF (polychromatic)
- Efficiency

**Constraints:**
- Fabrication limits (minimum feature size)
- Polarization sensitivity
- Cost

### Performance

**Chromatic correction:** < 5 μm F-C (vs. 50 μm for singlet)

**Efficiency:** > 80% (vs. < 50% for pure diffractive)

**MTF:** > 0.5 at 100 lp/mm (all wavelengths)

**Advantages:**
- Compact
- Broadband
- High performance

**Challenges:**
- Fabrication complexity
- Alignment
- Cost

## Summary

These case studies demonstrate:
- **Systematic design:** Requirements → paraxial → optimization
- **Analysis methods:** ABCD matrices, ray tracing, MTF
- **Optimization:** Merit functions, constraints, tolerancing
- **Performance evaluation:** Wavefront error, MTF, efficiency

Each design requires balancing multiple constraints: performance, size, cost, manufacturability. The tools and methods developed in previous modules enable systematic approach to these complex design problems.
